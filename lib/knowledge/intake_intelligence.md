# Intake Intelligence

A condensed guide for the intake agent. Use this to ask the right questions and generate contextually relevant options — not to interpret or analyze.

## How Symptoms Connect

Health issues cluster around three interconnected systems. Symptoms that seem unrelated often share a root cause. When someone mentions one symptom, related symptoms from the same cluster are worth probing.

**Gut health** → digestion, skin, mood, energy, brain fog, sleep, allergies, congestion, weight, immune function
**Stress response** → sleep, energy, mood, appetite, weight, digestion, hair, urination, temperature regulation
**Thyroid / energy metabolism** → energy, temperature, brain fog, weight, hair/nail quality, digestion, mood, sleep

These overlap heavily. Someone with fatigue, brain fog, and cold hands likely has a thyroid-metabolic pattern. Someone with digestive issues, skin problems, and mood changes likely has a gut-driven pattern. Someone with sleep disruption, irritability, and appetite changes likely has a stress-driven pattern. Most people have involvement across multiple systems.

## Symptom Probing Guide

For each complaint category, these are the specific details that differentiate one pattern from another.

### Energy / Fatigue

- **Timing matters most:**
  - Afternoon crashes → stress hormone dependence + gut-derived endotoxin
  - Morning fatigue → thyroid or cortisol pattern
  - Post-meal crashes → blood sugar regulation, gut bacterial fermentation
  - Post-exercise exhaustion → metabolic inefficiency, mitochondrial issue
  - All-day low-grade → systemic (thyroid, chronic stress, or gut inflammation)
- **Related probes:** cold extremities, brain fog, hair/nail quality, temperature sensitivity (thyroid cluster); crashes tied to meals (metabolic/gut cluster)

### Sleep

- **When they wake matters:**
  - First few hours → stress hormones (adrenaline, cortisol, estrogen)
  - Later in the night → gut disturbance (undigested food, bacteria stimulating nervous system)
  - To urinate → nutrient status (zinc, vitamin C, copper) or disrupted sleep signaling
  - To eat → poor liver function, nighttime blood sugar drops
  - For no apparent reason → intestinal irritation
- **Other sleep details that matter:**
  - Hot/sweaty at night → rising estrogen
  - Not dreaming → shallow, non-restorative sleep
  - Nightmares → nighttime stress hormones (cortisol, serotonin, noradrenaline)
  - Trouble falling asleep → light exposure, stress hormones, poor schedule
  - Sleep feels unrefreshing → excess serotonin (drowsy but not restorative)

### Digestion

- **Timing relative to meals is the key differentiator:**
  - Symptoms immediately after eating → stomach issue (low acid, poor motility)
  - Symptoms 1-3 hours after eating → small intestine issue (SIBO, poor bile/enzymes)
  - Relief after bowel movement → stool-related stress mediators (serotonin)
- **Specific symptoms differentiate:**
  - Bloating (subjective fullness) → upper GI bacterial gas + hypothyroidism sensitivity
  - Distention (visible expansion) → colonic gas + constipation/incomplete evacuation
  - Reflux → inflammation (histamine, serotonin, dysbiosis)
  - Belching → microbes + low stomach acid/bile/enzymes
  - Nausea → low stomach acid, gallbladder, overgrowths, or hormonal sensitivity
  - Diarrhea → stress hormones, elevated serotonin, bacterial overgrowth
- **Simple gut health indicators:**
  - Coated tongue (especially back) → bacterial/fungal overgrowth
  - Foamy urine → stress, bacterial overgrowth, detox activity
  - Dental problems (bad breath, cavities, gum issues) → mouth reflects intestinal microbes

### Weight / Body Composition

- **Where weight accumulates differentiates:**
  - Hips and belly → excess estrogen
  - Protruding belly specifically → liver issue or high cortisol
  - Soft, puffy fat (water retention) → estrogen
  - Cellulite → estrogen
- **Unintended weight loss** → bacterial/fungal overgrowth interfering with absorption
- **Related probes:** appetite changes, digestive symptoms, energy levels

### Mood / Mental Health

- **Specific patterns differentiate:**
  - Brain fog / poor clarity → hypothyroidism, neuroinflammation, high serotonin/lactic acid
  - Irritability / aggression → estrogen, serotonin, stress hormones, lactic acid
  - Loss of pleasure (anhedonia) → high serotonin, low dopamine
  - Hypervigilance / jitteriness / restlessness → elevated stress hormones
  - Anxiety → gut-brain axis disruption, stress hormones, serotonin
  - Depression → gut inflammation, serotonin/dopamine disruption
- **Key connection:** mental health issues are consistently associated with digestive issues — always worth probing gut symptoms when mood is mentioned

### Hormonal

- **Women-specific:**
  - Painful periods → excess estrogen
  - Irregular cycles / PMS → excess estrogen
  - Symptoms worsen at specific cycle phases → hormonal sensitivity
- **General hormonal markers:**
  - Skin issues (acne, eczema, psoriasis) → estrogen or gut-derived endotoxin
  - Hair loss, brittle nails → estrogen or thyroid
  - Sugar cravings → HPA axis activation (blood sugar drops triggering stress response)
  - Low libido / low drive → gut inflammation suppressing testosterone/dopamine

### Stress Indicators

- **Physical markers:**
  - Cold extremities → hypothyroidism
  - Resting pulse below 70 → may suggest hypothyroidism
  - Resting pulse above 90 → stress hormone activity
  - Morning temperature below 97.8°F → reliable hypothyroidism indicator
  - Frequent urination → fight-or-flight hormones elevated
  - Hair loss or premature graying → chronic stress
- **These are simple yes/no probes that provide high diagnostic signal with minimal effort**

### Skin

- **Patterns that differentiate:**
  - Acne → gut permeability, microbial imbalance, systemic inflammation
  - Eczema → impaired gut barrier, dysbiosis
  - Psoriasis → intestinal permeability (often co-occurs with GI symptoms)
  - Dandruff → microbial imbalance (gut-scalp connection)
  - Dry skin → may reflect hormonal or nutritional factors
- **Key connection:** skin issues often improve when gut health is addressed, even when digestive symptoms aren't prominent

### Allergies / Immune

- **Patterns:**
  - New or worsening allergies → compromised gut barrier, immune dysregulation
  - Chronic congestion (without mucus) → low carbon dioxide production
  - Food sensitivities → intestinal permeability
  - Histamine-type reactions → mast cell activation from gut inflammation

## Cross-System Patterns

When multiple symptoms from different categories appear together, they often point to the same root system:

- **Fatigue + cold hands + brain fog + hair issues** → thyroid-metabolic pattern
- **Digestive issues + skin problems + mood changes** → gut-driven pattern
- **Sleep disruption + irritability + appetite changes + weight gain** → stress-driven pattern
- **Fatigue + digestive issues + mood + sleep** → likely multi-system, gut and stress overlapping
- **Everything seems connected / nothing has worked** → energy metabolism (the common upstream cause)

## Diet & Lifestyle Patterns Worth Probing

These are counter-intuitive in the bioenergetic framework — common approaches that may be contributing to the very symptoms someone is trying to fix. When someone mentions these, probe for changes since starting:

- **Low-carb / keto / carnivore / fasting / caloric restriction** → can act as metabolic stressors. Probe: changes in energy, temperature, sleep, cravings, mood since starting.
- **Seed oil / PUFA-heavy diet** → can suppress metabolic rate. Most processed and restaurant food is cooked in seed oils.
- **Irregular meal timing / skipping meals** → activates stress response via blood sugar drops.
- **Low salt intake** → activates stress systems.
- **Limited sunlight exposure** → affects thyroid function, vitamin D, circadian rhythm, stress hormones.

## What Makes an Answer Specific vs. Vague

**Vague:** "I have low energy"
**Specific:** "I crash every afternoon after lunch, and exercise makes me feel worse not better"

**Vague:** "I've tried everything"
**Specific:** "I did keto for 6 months, tried various B vitamins, saw a naturopath who put me on an elimination diet"

**Vague:** "My digestion is bad"
**Specific:** "I get bloated within 30 minutes of eating, especially after carbs, and I have a coated tongue most mornings"

The goal is to help users move from vague to specific — through contextually relevant options and targeted follow-up questions.
